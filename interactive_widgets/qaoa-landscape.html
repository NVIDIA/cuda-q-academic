QAOA Optimization Landscape

Explore the 3D cost landscape of a 1-layer QAOA circuit, see how tuning parameters helps the optimizer find the optimal Max Cut, and discover why optimization gets harder at scale—the barren plateau problem.

Instructions

  1. Rotate the 3D landscape using your mouse to view the peaks and valleys.
  2. Use the Cost and Mixer sliders to move your current state across the landscape.
  3. Observe how the Expectation Value changes. Your goal is to navigate to the lowest possible point (the darkest valley).
  4. Watch the Probability bar to see how finding a lower expectation value increases your chances of measuring the optimal Max Cut.
  5. Increase the Barren plateau slider to flatten the landscape and see why optimization becomes difficult in larger or deeper circuits.

Keyboard: Tab to move between controls, ← → arrow keys to adjust sliders. Tab to the 3D view and use arrow keys to rotate.

What is a barren plateau? In bigger or deeper circuits the cost landscape can become almost flat, so the optimizer has no clear direction to improve. The slider above simulates that effect.

In code, the cudaq_solvers.qaoa function uses an optimizer like COBYLA to automatically adjust these sliders for you, searching for the lowest point on the graph to the right.
