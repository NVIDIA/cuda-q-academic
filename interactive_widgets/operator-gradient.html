Choosing the Next Operator

One QAOA layer has already been applied to a triangle graph (α = 0.60, β = 0.40). Now you can add one more operator. Which one helps the most? The gradient tells you.

Instructions
  • Pick an operator from the five buttons below. Each one changes the quantum state differently.
  • Drag the θ slider. At θ = 0 the operator is off (not applied). As you increase θ, the operator acts more strongly — watch the histogram and energy change.
  • Look at the E(θ) plot. The gradient is the slope of this curve at θ = 0 (the dashed tangent line). A steep downward slope means the energy drops fast when you first turn on the operator — that's a large gradient.
  • Compare operators by clicking each one. The one with the steepest slope at θ = 0 would be the best to add next.

Keyboard: Tab between controls, arrow keys to adjust θ, ESC to reset.
