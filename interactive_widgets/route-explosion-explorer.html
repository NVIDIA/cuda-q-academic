Instructions
  1. Use the Number of Stops slider to choose how many delivery locations the drone must visit (2–7).
  2. Click Play to watch the drone fly through every possible route, one by one.
  3. Adjust Speed to control how fast routes are animated.
  4. Watch the route counter climb — notice how adding just one stop multiplies the number of routes.
  5. Check the Factorial Explosion table to see why this problem becomes impossible for even the fastest computers.

Keyboard: Tab to navigate controls. Enter / Space to activate buttons. ← → to adjust sliders. Esc to reset.

Why does this matter? This is the Traveling Salesperson Problem — one of the most famous challenges in computer science. Finding the shortest route means checking it against every other possibility. With just 30 delivery stops, those possibilities number 2.7 × 1032 — a number so large that checking one route per nanosecond would still take longer than the age of the universe. As you saw, even enumerating every route for 7 stops takes real time — and the number grows factorially from there. No computer can brute-force its way through, which is why researchers develop smart heuristic algorithms, and why quantum computing may offer fundamentally new approaches to optimization.
