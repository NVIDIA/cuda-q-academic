QAOA Max Cut Explorer

See QAOA working on the simplest interesting graph: a triangle (3 nodes, 3 edges). Each node gets one qubit, so there are 2³ = 8 possible colorings. You play the role of the classical optimizer — two sliders control the quantum circuit's Cost angle (α), which encodes the graph structure, and Mixer angle (β), which creates quantum interference between different colorings.

Instructions
  • Start at α = 0, β = 0. All 8 colorings are equally likely (12.5% each) — the qubits are in a uniform superposition. This is akin to pure random guessing on a classical computer.
  • Drag the sliders. Watch the histogram shift: blue bars (good cuts that cut 2 edges) grow while gray bars (bad cuts that cut 0 edges) shrink or vice versa. This is quantum interference at work.
  • Press "Sample (Measure)." The superposition collapses to one definite coloring — just as it would on a quantum computer when measuring the qubits.
  • Hover over any bar to preview that coloring on the triangle.

Keyboard: Use Tab to move between controls. Use Arrow Keys to adjust sliders. Press Enter to Sample. Press ESC to reset to 0.

▶ What does the ⟨H⟩ badge mean?

The ⟨H⟩ badge shows the expectation value of the Max Cut Hamiltonian — a weighted average of the cut score across all possible measurement outcomes. Each cut edge contributes −1, so more negative = better cut.

At α = β = 0 (uniform superposition), the two “all same color” states (000, 111) each score 0 and the six good-cut states each score −2, giving ⟨H⟩ = 2 × 0.125 × 0 + 6 × 0.125 × (−2) = −1.50.

Challenge: Can you push ⟨H⟩ all the way to −2.00? That means the circuit is concentrating all probability onto maximum cuts.
